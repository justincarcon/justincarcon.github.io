What We Offer

Expert advice on BC EA Certificates

The wording in EA Certificates must be easy to understand and practical to implement. Justin Carlson Consulting Corporation can provide advice and guidance in the development process of conditions, or assistance in the interpretation and implementation of these certificates.

Compliance with EA Certificates


Compliance with EA Certificates can be challenging. The language is often open to interpretation which can lead to surprising revelations when inspected by regulatory agencies. This, coupled with increased public scrutiny and rising costs in labour and materials can lead to costly delays and damaged reputation. Justin Carlson Consulting Corporation, and its partners, can help your Project in the regulatory interpretation of Certificate requirements to potentially avoid or mitigate costly enforcement.

The three most common non-compliances on major Projects in BC seem to centre around erosion sediment control, attractants/waste and spills. Regulators are always on the lookout for these items and failure to ensure that these items are addressed in manner suitable to regulators can lead to costly delays and impacts to public image. Justin Carlson Consulting Corporation and its partners can aid in assessing and reducing regulatory risk through a solutions based approach. In addition to the above, Justin Carlson Consulting Corporation can also review your regulatory authorizations and provide advice or guidance on meeting those requirements and lessening your compliance risk.

Clear and Practical Management Plans

Regulators are often inspecting against the mitigation contained within management plans that are tied to EA Certificate requirements. Vague or impractical language can often lead to enforcement. We can provide you with advice on regulatory interpretations and guidance to potentially prevent or mitigate non-compliances and enforcement. We can review draft plans prior to approval, or plans after the approval for potential risk and solutions.

About Us

Justin Carlson Consulting Corporation is a unique consulting firm specializing in projects subject to the BC Environmental Assessment Act. Our founder has worked almost exclusively on EAO projects for over 12 years. We've worked with industry clients, and within the EAO in environmental compliance for the past 12 years.

Justin Carlson

Justin Carlson is the firm's founder and Principal Consultant. He is an experienced professional in the environmental compliance field with a specialty in major projects in British Columbia.
Justin began his career in government almost 20 years ago as a regulator. He now works for industry as a consultant. He was the first initial hire into the EAO C&E program in 2012 and was instrumental in the development and implementation of EAO C&E. Today, Justin brings significant EAO expertise coupled with industry consulting experience to any potential client. With his experience, he can help clients navigate the regulatory landscape of major Projects in British Columbia.

Key Experience

Justin aided in the development and implementation of the EAO Compliance and Enforcement Program including providing suggestions and direct input to their policies, procedures and updated legislation that guide the EAO Compliance and Enforcement branch today.

He is currently specializing in the interface between owner, regulators and Prime contractors.

As EAO’s Senior Compliance and Enforcement Officer, Justin worked with industry leaders and regulatory partners to advance compliance on over 40 major projects, including the Site C Dam, Pacific Trails Pipeline, LNG Canada, Brule Mine, Willow Creek Mine, Wolverine Mine, Orca Sand and Gravel, Roman Coal Mine, Coastal GasLink Pipeline, Woodfibre LNG, Upper Lillooet Hydro, Meikle Wind, Bear Mountain Wind, Quality Wind, Kokish Hydroelectric, Silverberry Hazardous Waste Facility, Northern Rockies Hazardous Waste Facility, Port Mann/Highway 1, Evergreen Rapid Transit, Vancouver Airport Fuel Delivery, Northwest Transmission Line, Chemanius Wells, and Forrest Kerr Hydro.
